Bonus Values Across All Cards:

Principles that these cards currently use:

- Cards are divided by keyframe sections - Dividing by keyframe doesn't look at how Well you did it, just how much motion you chose to have in your piece.
- Keyframe sections must consist of at least 2 keys and one inbetween
- If a piece only has 2 keys and no inbetweens, or doesn't animate a full body section (ie, a blinking that only redraws the eyes) it gets a regular art card with "small animated flair"
- If a piece has "combined-transform" frames, they cannot count towards being inbetweens (pieces must be at least broken apart)
- If a piece reverses the action to form a loop, it can't count as a new section unless there is something obviously different going on
- "Animation principles" is a vague bonus right now, but based on Dips' idea of maybe combining some of the proposed things into a single bonus
- "3 or more hand-drawn" bonus does NOT count the keys in that section, only the frames between it. If there are more than 3 inbetweens that are handdrawn this applies on top of the traditional bonus - it helps to balance traditional if a player is uncertain about their keyframe sections; if they break it down into super small chunks this won't apply - but if they break it into bigger chunks then this helps keep the balance so that a single piece won't be drastically different in points based on how the keyframe sections are defined.

Additionally:

Keep in mind that MP cards being large doesn't have to mean exponential work for mods.

Each piece has "sub-cards" within - we can ask players to submit each sub-card as its own card if we think it would be better for mod health/ease.
